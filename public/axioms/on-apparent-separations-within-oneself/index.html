On Apparent Separations Within Oneself Tuesday, December 31, 2024

I previously believed that there was a distinction between the mind and the soul. Though these entities are ill-defined, my understanding was (though obviously wrong in hindsight) that they are almost orthogonal– I believed that the mind had little to no bearing on the soul and vice-versa. I no longer believe this; I believe this separation was an illusion.

This was exemplified by my view on my work and my hobbies:

  • My academics, my mathematics, would serve my mind and make me feel intellectually fulfilled.
  • My hobbies, such as poetry and cloud watching, would serve my soul and make me feel “warm and fuzzy”, for lack of a better term.
  • My academics then could not be spiritually fulfilling and, similarly, my hobbies could not be intellectually fulfilling.
