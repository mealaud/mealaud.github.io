I used to operate off of my axioms and I will once more. Before I was compelled to use axioms as I was obsessed with mathematics, but now I _choose_ to conduct myself this way as a way to institute structure. In other words, this is a means to my greatest end——a better life.{{%nl%}}{{%nl%}}

My axioms will be stated below in order. Below them are the propositions derived from these axioms.

## Axioms
1. There is no freedom without restraint.
2. I am the principle bearer of the burden of my life.
3. It's not a matter of if I can, I must.
4. I must work to improve (in all aspects of myself).

## Propositions 
{{% MathEnv "prop" %}}
My life can only markedly improve and stay improved only if I, myself, make an effort.
{{% /MathEnv %}}
{{% Proof %}}
If my life did improve according to random chance it is not necessarily permanent. Such a random change may indeed be, once again, randomly taken away. If I am to make a sustained, positive change in my life, I must be able to control whether this change continues to take place. I must be the one deciding whether to continue instituting this change. Hence I must decide. Therefore, I must make an effort.
{{% /Proof %}}
{{% MathEnv "rem" %}}
Someone else may help you in this process, or even start it (say a friend urging you to go the doctor or therapy), but you must make the final say in whether anything happens at all. You are always the crux of the attempt, though someone else may be a central aspect or instigator of it.
{{% /MathEnv %}}

{{% MathEnv "prop" %}}
My word must have worth and, if it does not, I can ensure it will.
{{% /MathEnv %}}
{{% MathEnv "rem" %}}
I have made many empty promises to myself in the past (whether I was aware or not). Each time I became aware of a such a _lie_ I doubt my ability to change, to improve, to continue. This must not be possible. My word must have worth so that I can keep promises to myself (and to others).
{{% /MathEnv %}}
{{% Proof %}}
We suppose without loss of generality that my word _does not_ have worth. (As if it did we would be done!) In this case, what can be done to amend it? Like Proposition \#1 states, this can only improve if I make a continuous effort, hence I will suppose my word does and act to make this true. 
{{% /Proof %}}
{{% MathEnv "rem" %}}
In some sense, it is irrelevant whether your word means anything as you must act like it does to give it the hoped for worth. Roughly speaking, this is "fake it till you make it".
{{% /MathEnv %}}

{{% MathEnv "coro" %}}
What is life but a promise to oneself to live? I must be a man of my word. I must keep my promises. I must live.
{{% /MathEnv %}}

{{% MathEnv "prop" %}}
One must never resign.
{{% /MathEnv %}}
{{% Proof %}}
If one resigns to their situation (i.e., gives up and no longer puts in effort into their life), Proposition \#1 tells us that their life can no longer neither markedly improve and stay improved. Therefore we are violating Axiom \#4 and thus one cannot resign.
{{% /Proof %}}
