According to the {{<ref "definition of a topological manifold" "/notes/definition-of-a-topological-manifold">}}, an $ m $-dimensional topological manifold $ M^m $ is locally homeomorphic to the standard $ \mathbb{R}^m $. {{%nl%}}{{%nl%}}

Locally a (topological) manifold looks just like Euclidean space! This is a big part of why manifolds are cool (to my knowledge).
