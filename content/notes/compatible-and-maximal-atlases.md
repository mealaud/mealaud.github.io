A local chart $ (U,x) $ on $ M $ is said to be {{% tdf "compatible" %}}  with a {{<ref "$ C^r $-atlas" "/notes/definition-of-an-atlas">}} $ \mathcal{A} $ if the union $ \mathcal{A} \cup \left \\{ (U,x) \right \\}  $ is a $ C^r $-atlas.{{%nl%}}{{%nl%}}

A $ C^r $-atlas $ \widehat{\mathcal{A}} $ is said to be {{% tdf "maximal" %}} if it contains all local charts that are compatible with it.
{{% /MathEnv %}}

I imagine the existence of maximal atlases come from Zorn's Lemma, similar to how maximal ideals exist in rings.
