Recall {{<ref "the moral of differentiation" "/notes/the-moral-of-differentiation">}}: 
1. Differentiation is a local property.
2. Differentiation isn't always possible.
3. When differentiation is possible, it simplifies things.

The reason we care about manifolds is because its allows us to use the concepts of differentiation on spaces themselves! Indeed, consider yourself on Earth. Where you're standing right now likely has a "flat" ground. Flat stuff, like planes, are very nice and easy to understand, but something like the surface of the sphere Earth is, can be a bit more difficult. Applying the morals of differentiation to spaces we've arrived at the fact that some spaces admit simpler objects that represent them locally! And, in our schemes, this "simpler object" is just some part of Euclidean space. We put this in a graphic:
