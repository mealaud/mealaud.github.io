In Isaacs, he states three equivalent statements to the "definition" of state variables we gave in {{<ref "How to play games" "/notes/how-to-play-games">}}. These three equivalent statements are:
1. State variables are values that must be known at the outset of the game in order to determine the outcome of the game.
    - Honestly, I'm confused about what state variables implies here as I thought state variables just determine the instant they are in reference to, not the entirety of the game. Like with tag, everyone starts standing still, but each game of tag is different. Not really sure what to make of this equivalent statement. It seems to go from local to global properties which is really odd.
2. State variables are exactly the values that are relevant at each instant of a game to the player for the player to make decisions.
    - This aligns more with the statement in the last post.
3. If a player had to be replaced by a substitute during a game, the information the substitute would need to resume play be the state variables at that instant. 
    - This one confused me like number 1. haha
