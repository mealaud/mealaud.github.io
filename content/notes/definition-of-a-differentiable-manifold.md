A {{<ref "maximal atlas" "/notes/compatible-and-maximal-atlases">}} $ \widehat{A} $ on $ M $ is also called a {{% tdf "$ C^r $-structure" %}} on $ M $. The pair $ (M, \widehat A) $ is said to be a {{% tdf "$ C^r $-manifold" %}}, or a {{% tdf "differentiable manifold" %}} of class $ C^r $, if $ M $ is {{<ref "topological manifold" "/notes/definition-of-a-topological-manifold">}} and $ \widehat A $ is a $ C^r $-structure on $ M $. A differentiable manifold is said to be {{% tdf "smooth" %}} if its transition maps are $ C^\infty $.
